Questions

What does Claims Auditor check for?
Two things. First, substantiation: unsourced statistics, superlatives like "the best" or "the leading," authority and award claims with no proof, absolute guarantees, and risky assertions on high-stakes topics, each mapped to the E-E-A-T pillar it weakens. Second, with fact-check turned on, accuracy and recency: it verifies concrete facts, stats, dates, and prices against current sources and flags anything inaccurate or outdated.
Does it check whether facts are accurate or just unsourced?
Both, when you enable fact-check. It searches current reliable sources and labels each finding as inaccurate (conflicts with what sources now say), outdated (was true once but superseded), or unsubstantiated (simply unsupported on the page). When it cannot confirm something is wrong, it does not claim it is. It flags it as "worth verifying" with low confidence so you can check, and it never invents a source.
What is E-E-A-T?
Google's framework for judging content quality: Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness. It shapes how both search and AI assistants decide whether your pages are credible enough to rank and cite.
How do credits work?
A standard audit uses 3 credits per page. A fact-checked audit uses 6 credits per page, because verifying facts against live sources does more work. Crawl mode audits several pages at once and charges per page. You only pay for pages that actually run, never for ones that error or get blocked.
Do credits expire?
No. Buy once and use them whenever you like. There is no subscription and no monthly reset.
What if a site blocks the audit?
Some sites use bot protection. Switch to Single URL or Paste text mode and the audit runs on the content directly. For your own or client sites, the crawler can be allowlisted on request.
Can I download the report?
Yes. Export any audit as a CSV for spreadsheets or a formatted PDF to share with your client or team. Fact-check findings, current-source notes, and confidence levels are included.
